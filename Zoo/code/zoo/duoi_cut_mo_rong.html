    Psarisomus dalhousiae

    Psarisomus dalhousiae is a species of bird in the Eurylaimidae family.Psarisomus dalhousiae lives in Hymalaya, Southeast Asia. The adult bird has a dark green color, the head and neck are black, the sides of the neck have two yellow streaks. Turmeric face, throat, necklace. The primary and secondary wing feathers are black, green and white at sight. The tail feathers are long and narrow, dark green above and black below. Young birds have a darker color and blue heads Adult birds have dark green fur, heads and nape are black

    Habitat & Ecology

    Birds live and nest in the jungle where the altitude is less than 1500 - 1800m. Often found foraging and nesting on the upper floors of the forest.

    Distribution

    Vietnam: From the Viet Trung border to Thua Thien - Hue. South of the Central region is the distribution of the second subspecies (P. d. Divinus). World: Hymalaia, Myanmar, southern China, Indochina, Thailand.

                   

    Hydrornis elliotii

    Hydrornis elliotii is a species of bird in the Pittidae family.

    Describe

    19.5-21cm. Adult males: Forehead, head crown and green nape. A wide black band extends from the front of the eye to the back of the back of the black nape. The back has the same blue color. The face of the abdomen has a horizontal stripe, except for a strip running along the center in deep purple-green color. Black beak. Female: The belly is more yellow. The top of the head and the back of the head are brown. Young bird: Close to the female, brown and spotted.

    Distribution

    This species is found in Southeast Asia, throughout the country (except for the Red River Delta and the Southwest).

    Current Status

    Endemic species of Indochina. Relatively common in some distribution areas.

    Habitat

    Types of forests and bamboo. Can meet up to about 800 meters high, but mostly found in low belts, such as Ke Go forest, Ha Tinh. Breeding around May 4-7, often found nesting in rattan, coniferous leaves. Lay 3-4 eggs.
