Irena puella

Irena puella is a species of bird in Irenidae.

Distribution

This bird is found in forests across tropical South Asia from the foot of Mount Hymalaya, India and Sri Lanka east through Indochina, the Big Sunda Islands and Palawan (Philippines).

Features & Reproduction

They lay 2 or 3 eggs in a small bowl-shaped nest in a tree. It was described by British bird researcher John Latham in 1790. Adults are about 24–27 cm long. The male has a glossy upper part, iridescent blue, and a black lower part and flying feathers.

Chloropsis palawanensis

Chloropsis palawanensis is a species of bird in the Green Bird family.

Describe

The male is easily recognizable by the burnt orange-colored lower body. The female is light blue, below the ears are mauve and silvery-green, black under the eyes. Young birds are completely green. Chirping: Many volumes, such as Cheo, can mimic the calls of other birds in the area.

Distribution

The yellow-footed blue bird is endemic to the Palawan region of the Philippines. Its natural habitat is subtropical or tropical moist rainforest. From the North to the Central of our country.

Residence & Food

Chloropsis palawanensis is commonly found in forests, forest margins, and shrub forests. It uses its slender pointed beak to eat insects and small fruits in the forest canopy. It is a fairly common common species endemic to Palawan. Chloropsis palawanensis can be found in Balabac, Busuanga, Calauit, Coron, Culion, Dumaran, and mainland Palawan..
