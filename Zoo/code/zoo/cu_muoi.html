    Batrachostomus hodgsoni

    Batrachostomus hodgsoni belongs to B. hodgsoni This is a night bird. They possess a cruel coat to blend easily with the color of dry leaves or bark.

    Distribution

    In the world, the giant mosquito is distributed in some countries such as Malaysia, Southeast Thailand, Bangladesh, Bhutan, China, India, Laos ...In Vietnam, the giant mosquito distributes Kontum, Bien Hoa, Thu Dau Mot and is quite common in Tay Ninh forest.               

    Characteristics

    The male batrachostomus hodgsoni has a dark brown hung back with narrow black stripes. The top of the head has thick dark brown stripes and stripes, while the breast has a dark and dark brown stripe. The female Batrachostomus hodgsoni has a completely brownish fur, the back is slightly dark and the face is slightly pale, across the chest is a white band of black border, pale pink bill.

    Residence & Food

    Batrachostomus hodgsoni usually do not make nests but lay eggs in tree cavities or on dry leaf clusters on the ground. The food of the giant mosquito is mainly insects and mosquitoes.            

    Reproduction

    Batrachostomus hodgsoni lays 1 litter / 1 year, each litter of 1-4 eggs.
